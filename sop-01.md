# Guide 01: Creating Clean, Custom Websites & Hosting for Free

The goal of this guide is to show how anyone can create and manage their own secure, personal websites without getting locked into expensive monthly hosting contracts. By separating where you buy your name from where you store your files, you can bring your running costs down to pennies a day.

---

## 1. Choosing the Right Path for Your Idea
Depending on what kind of website you want to make, different free systems work best:

*   **Photography / Art Portfolios (e.g., simeon.pics):** Build a clean layout webpage, store your high-quality files inside a shared Google Photos album link, and let visitors look, heart, and comment natively using their personal accounts.
*   **Simple Writing / Project Blogs (e.g., simbo.tech):** Write simple layout documents and save them in a public folder on GitHub to host text and tutorials cleanly.
*   **Personal Identity Pages (e.g., simeonpaynter.com):** Create a basic introduction layout and hook up a free forms connector to let friends email you straight from the browser.

## 2. Step-by-Step Implementation

### Phase A: Freeing Your Domain Name From Costly Bundles
1. Log into your current website provider account (such as WordPress).
2. Go to your active upgrades list and turn your monthly subscription auto-renew off. (Do not cancel the account entirely, or you will lose your domain name).
3. Find your domain name list, select your web address, and turn the domain "Transfer Lock" off.
4. Click the button to request your Transfer Authorization Code (also called an EPP code). Copy the sequence sent to your inbox.

### Phase B: Pointing Your Domain Safely at Porkbun
1. Log into your Porkbun account and head to the "Pending Transfers" dashboard [1.1].
2. Enter your domain name and paste your unique Authorization Code [1.1]. Submit and pay the base processing fee (this automatically adds a full extra year to your expiration date).
3. Look for the "DNS Records" menu next to your domain name [1.1]. Scroll to the bottom and select the built-in "GitHub" option [1.1].
4. Set the host field to "www" and type your main code account location (username.github.io) into the answer box [1.1]. Click submit [1.1].

### Phase C: Storing and Launching Your Pages on GitHub
1. Create a free account on GitHub and build a new, "Public" project folder named exactly after your domain name.
2. Check the box to add a standard introduction text page and an open-source MIT License.
3. Create a master webpage document named precisely: index.html. Save your custom layout code here.
4. Click on your folder Settings > Pages. Set the folder engine to deploy from your master branch and click Save.
5. Paste your root website address (e.g., simbo.tech) into the Custom Domain text box and click Save. 
6. Give the system 10 minutes to verify everything, then check the box to mandatory Enforce HTTPS (securing the little padlock icon in your visitor's browser bar).

## 3. Financial Cost Transparency & Ledger
To keep things completely clear, here is the exact out-of-pocket list tracking my initial setup. I chose to pre-pay my personal photography and writing domains for a full 10 years upfront to avoid domain name inflation. 

*   **Personal Portfolio (simeonpaynter.com):** $11.00 upfront // $11.00 a year recurring. [1.2]
*   **Photography Feed (simeon.pics):** $237.88 upfront (Fully paid up for the next 10 years).
*   **Writing & Technical Lab (simbo.tech):** $465.81 upfront (Fully paid up for the next 10 years).
*   **Website Server Space (GitHub Pages):** $0.00 completely free hosting.
*   **Image Cloud Storage (Google Photos):** $0.00 completely free image limits.
*   **SSL padlocks & Account Privacy:** $0.00 permanently included free via Porkbun.
*   **TOTAL UPFRONT INVESTMENT:** $714.69
*   **ONGOING RECURRING RUN RATE:** Only $11.00 / year for the next 9 years! [1.2]

*Note on Domain Promos: Many domain names feature very cheap introductory sale prices for year one. Always look closely at the standard standard renewal costs before buying an extension so you aren't surprised by price changes later on.*
